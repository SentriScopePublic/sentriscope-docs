# Canonical Understanding

## Definition

**Canonical understanding** is **shared, explainable security meaning** that an organization can defend in decisions—built by normalizing observations into common entity types, linking related signals, and stating confidence where linkage is uncertain.

Where [canonical data](./CANONICAL_DATA.md) describes **normalized records**, canonical understanding describes the **coherent picture** analysts and managers act on: which assets, identities, exposures, and intelligence belong to the same story, and why that story matters now.

Canonical understanding enables correlation across tools without requiring every analyst to manually reconcile proprietary formats for every decision.
