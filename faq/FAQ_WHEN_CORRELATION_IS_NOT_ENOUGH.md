# When is correlation not enough?

## Short answer

Correlation is **not enough** when the linked story lacks supporting evidence, relies on wrong or uncertain entity linkage, uses stale relationships, or when the decision requires **validation**, **explicit uncertainty**, or **business judgment** that no correlated view can supply alone.

## Explanation

A well-linked hypothesis is not a conclusion. Teams should add **evidence validation**, **stated unknowns**, and **proportional action** when:

- Observations may be missing from the story  
- Linkage confidence is low  
- Paths or dependencies changed since last update  
- Novel activity has no historical pattern to link  
- Executive or legal process requires more than technical correlation  

Correlation suggests where to look and what might belong together; it does not replace investigation discipline or proof.
