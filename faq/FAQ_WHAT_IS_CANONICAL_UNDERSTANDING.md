# What is canonical understanding?

## Short answer

**Canonical understanding** is the **shared, explainable security picture** an organization can defend when prioritizing, investigating, and communicating risk—built from normalized observations, correlated stories, relationship context, and stated confidence where linkage is uncertain.

## Explanation

[Canonical data](../glossary/CANONICAL_DATA.md) provides normalized records. Canonical understanding is the **meaning** analysts and managers act on: which entities are involved, which observations belong to the same story, and why that story matters now.

Normalization without correlation yields consistent fragments. Correlation without shared entity semantics yields unstable stories. Canonical understanding requires both—plus accountable judgment.
